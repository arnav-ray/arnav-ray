A reading frame

Pull and Push: How AI Platform Power Is Splitting in 2026

Two strategies are organising the agentic AI market. They look like rivals. They are increasingly collaborators, negotiating through a shared layer of standards.

Pull →
Also called aggregator or model-as-platform
Who
Anthropic OpenAI Google DeepMind
Mechanism
Frontier model at the centre. Apps, data, and tools are pulled in through MCP servers, connectors, and agents.
Where the user lives
Inside the model's interface. The chat surface, the API, the agent loop.
Strategic goal
Become the universal interface. Make the model the place work begins.
→ data and capabilities flow inward
← Push
Also called incumbent or app-as-platform
Who
SAP Salesforce Adobe
Mechanism
Application at the centre. Frontier models are pushed inside the platform as embedded copilots and domain agents.
Where the user lives
Inside the application. The system of record stays the home.
Strategic goal
Defend data gravity. Make the application the place AI is most useful.
← intelligence flows into the system of record
Where they meet

Negotiated intelligence through governed protocols

Pull and Push do not need to defeat each other. They need a shared interface so a generalist agent can act on a sovereign system without breaking either side's contract.

Open protocol
MCP
Common wire protocol for agents to discover tools and invoke actions across vendors.
Door policy
SAP API Policy v4
Only published APIs are sanctioned. Defines what an agent is allowed to touch inside the system.
Operating layer
Governance and audit
Who decided what, on whose behalf, with which data. The work that does not go away.
Reading frame, not industry vocabulary. Closer existing analogues: aggregator vs incumbent (Stratechery), model-as-platform vs app-as-platform.
